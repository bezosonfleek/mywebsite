History of Dodge

Dodge is an American automobile brand that has been around since 1900. It was founded by the Dodge Brothers, Horace and John Dodge. Initially, they started as a parts suppliers for other automakers, but in 1914, they decided to venture into manufacturing their own cars.One of Dodge's most iconic models is the Dodge Charger, which was first introduced in 1966. The Charger quickly gained popularity for its powerful performance and sleek design. It became a symbol of American muscle cars and has remained an enduring symbol of Dodge's commitment to performance and style.Dodge has also made a name for itself in the world of motorsports. The brand has a strong presence in NASCAR, with many successful racing teams and drivers representing Dodge on the track.Today, Dodge continues to produce a diverse lineup of vehicles, ranging from sporty cars like the Challenger and Charger to rugged SUVs like the Durango. The brand is known for its bold and aggressive design language, as well as its commitment to delivering high-performance vehicles.
